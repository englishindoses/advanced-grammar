Advanced ESL Grammar Course

Welcome to the Advanced English Grammar Course for adult ESL learners. This course is designed to help you deepen your understanding of advanced grammar and refine your ability to express yourself clearly and confidently in English. Each module builds on your existing knowledge, guiding you through more complex structures and helping you become a more fluent, sophisticated user of the language.

How to Use This Course

  • Each topic contains detailed explanations, examples, and practical exercises.
  • Complete the modules in order or focus on specific areas you wish to improve.
  • Practice exercises are available at the end of each section.
  • Regular assessments will help track your progress.
  • Extra resources and lessons are available.

Course Structure

Each topic will follow this format:

  1. Introduction - Overview of the grammatical concept
  2. Explanation - Detailed breakdown with rules and exceptions
  3. Examples - Authentic usage in various contexts
  4. Common Errors - Typical mistakes and how to avoid them
  5. Practice Exercises - Apply what you've learned
  6. Extension Activities - Deepen your understanding

Course Index

1. Advanced Tenses and Aspect

  • Present Perfect Progressive vs. Present Perfect Simple
  • Past Perfect Progressive vs. Past Perfect
  • Future Perfect vs. Future Perfect Continuous
  • Mixed Time Frames with Perfect Tenses
  • Advanced Uses of Present Perfect
  • Perfect Infinitives and Perfect Gerunds
  • Narrative Tenses (Past Perfect, Past Simple, Past Continuous)

2. Conditionals and Hypotheticals

  • Zero, First, Second, Third, and Mixed Conditionals
  • Inverted Conditionals (e.g., "Had I known...")
  • Double Conditionals
  • Conditional Expressions (Unless, Provided that, As long as)
  • Wish, If only, and Would rather for regret and desire

3. Modality

  • Modal Perfect Forms (must have been, could have done)
  • Modal Progressive Forms (must be doing, should be working)
  • Subtle Differences Between Similar Modals (might vs. may vs. could)
  • Advanced Modal Meanings (reproach, criticism, probability)
  • Mixed Modal Constructions

4. Passive Voice

  • Basic Passive Structures (Present and Past)
  • Advanced Passive Constructions with Get and Have
  • Passive Infinitives and Gerunds
  • Passive Reporting Verbs (is thought to be, is said to have)
  • Complex Passive Causatives (have/get something done)
  • Double Passive Constructions

5. Complex Sentence Structures

  • Relative Clauses (Defining, Non-Defining, with Prepositions, Reduced)
  • Nominal Clauses
  • Subjunctive Mood (Present, Past, Future)
  • Inversion for Emphasis (after negative adverbials, in conditionals)
  • Cleft Sentences (It is/was... that/who)
  • Participle Clauses (Present and Past)

6. Verb Patterns & Complementation

  • Verbs Followed by Gerund vs. Infinitive (with meaning changes)
  • Complex Infinitive Structures
  • Causative Verbs (make, let, have, get, help)
  • Advanced Reporting Verbs and Their Patterns

7. Adverbials & Prepositional Phrases

  • Adverb Position and Multiple Adverbs
  • Complex Prepositional Phrases
  • Dependent Prepositions with Verbs, Nouns, and Adjectives
  • Focusing Adverbs and Adverbials
  • Comment Adverbs and Attitude Markers

8. Noun Phrases & Articles

  • Complex Noun Phrases with Multiple Modifiers
  • Article Usage in Academic and Formal Writing
  • Generic vs. Specific Reference with Articles
  • Nominalization in Academic Writing
  • Quantifiers with Uncountable and Abstract Nouns

9. Advanced Modifiers

  • Gradability and Ungradability of Adjectives
  • Compound and Complex Adjectives
  • Adjective Order and Multiple Adjectives
  • Intensifiers and Downtoners
  • Advanced Comparative and Superlative Structures

10. Discourse & Cohesion

  • Advanced Discourse Markers and Linking Words
  • Ellipsis and Substitution
  • Reference Devices and Text Cohesion
  • Thematic Progression and Information Structure
  • Cohesive Devices in Academic Writing

11. Special Structures & Expressions

  • Emphatic Structures (do, does, did)
  • Tag Questions (including irregular forms)
  • Echo Questions and Question Tags
  • Fronting and Focusing Devices
  • Hedging Devices and Tentative Language

12. Register & Style

  • Formal vs. Informal Grammar Structures
  • Academic Writing Conventions
  • Literary and Rhetorical Devices
  • Idiomatic and Figurative Language
  • Style Shifting and Genre Appropriate Grammar
